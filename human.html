Human Practices

Through the use of a four-step, human-centred design process, we will liaise with pivotal people for our project conceptualization, design, evaluation, and improvement.

First, we will try to understand the problem by talking to major stakeholders, those who were involved with the problem of interest. Then, we will ideate solutions derived from synthetic biology and verified this need with the focal entities. Next, we will design solutions through expert consultations with academics. Lastly, we will evaluate and iterate on the design by hosting a faculty talk and consulting industry members to get feedback and improve our solution.

Understand the Problem

In order to gain a better understanding of water pollution and to elucidate the importance of maintaining a healthy environment, we decided to contact agricultural stakeholders because one of the largest allocations of surface water is irrigation on farms. Additionally, organizations that focus on water conservation and protection will help us better understand the implications of low chlorophyll levels on local water bodies.

The Western and Eastern Irrigation Districts of Alberta serve as a network to connect the public with farmers, water operations facilities, and administrative services that oversee our water distribution network.

Ideate

To address this problem, we propose the use of a water-soluble chlorophyll binding protein (6GIx) synthesized by genetically engineered bacteria to create a chlorophyll detector to measure levels in water bodies. However, to ascertain the need for our proposed solution, we would consult Environment Alberta and Environment and Climate Change Canada. These two organizations are key stakeholders in the conservation and environmental efforts occurring on both provincial and federal levels, respectively. Using their expertise would help us further develop our idea and understand how to integrate our technology into existing water management systems.

Evaluate and Iterate on Design

To do this, we will contact a local biotechnology company FREDsense, to evaluate our design and receive feedback on the logistics of our solution. Additionally, we propose hosting a symposium to present our novel method to local researchers, stakeholders, and organizations to elicit recommendations, so we can learn more on how to integrate our device into the existing water conservation ecosystem.
